ELECTROENCEPHALOGRAM (EEG) [BRAIN MEASUREMENT]

Definition: EEG (Electroencephalogram) is a non-invasive technique used for measuring and recording electrical activity in the brain.

How it Works: Electrodes are placed on the scalp, capturing electrical signals produced by neurons. These signals are translated into rhythmic wave patterns (alpha, beta, delta, theta), representing different cognitive states.

Purpose: EEG is valuable for understanding brain function, cognitive development, and detecting abnormalities. In the project, EEG is utilized to measure brain activity, providing insights into issues like fatigue and stress.

TAP ATTENTION TEST

Definition: TAP (Test of Attentional Performance) is a cognitive testing device used to assess various aspects of attention and cognitive functions.

How it Works: The TAP device involves tasks such as Alertness, Go/NoGo, and Divided Attention. These tasks evaluate participants' reaction times, alertness levels, and capacity to respond promptly to visual cues.

Purpose: The TAP Attention Test is utilized to assess cognitive performance and understand how attention levels may change over the course of neurofeedback sessions. It provides valuable data on alertness, reaction times, and attention capabilities.

NEUROFEEDBACK [BRAIN TRAINING]

Definition: Neurofeedback is a therapeutic technique that aims to train individuals to self-regulate and modulate their brain activity through real-time feedback.

How it Works: During neurofeedback sessions, clients observe their brain activity visualized on a screen in real-time. The goal is to enhance specific brain patterns associated with improved mental health.

Purpose: Neurofeedback is employed as a training tool to help individuals self-regulate their brain activity. It is utilized in the project to empower patients to reach desired levels of brain function and address conditions like stress, anxiety, and depression.
